AllTrail Redesign

Overview

This is a redesign project of a mobile application, AllTrails, that seeking outdoor trails. The purpose of the redesign is to elevate the UX of 3 main pages, aims to facilitate searching of eligible trails without being bothered by any verbose information request.

Approach

I have given a new look to the style of this app, including typeface theme color and buttons; simplified some pages by removing insignificant information, twisted the order and method of illustrating of the information.

Changes on the landing page

Changes & Improvements

1. The first version of the content showed in the info window was texts and a tiny picture. The problem is that in the relatively small window contains too many details, which gives labored reading experience. Based on the research of indirect competitors’ work, such as yelp, kayak, and Airbnb. I found that on the map browsing page, location is the primary info that users care about. The second is the info that facilitates searching of suitable targets. Here I choose to show the distance of the trail loop.

2. The position of the view shifting button has been tweaked a few times. In most of the popular apps, the view shifting button has been presented as a segment button or an icon on the nav bar at either left or right side. Since the smartphone has been designed in larger size, it becomes less easy to reach the top area for one-hand operation. Eventually, I decided to place the view shifting button at the left bottom, a spot that could be comfortably reached by a thumb.

3. The zoom level of the map is a detail I have found, that has been missed by some competitors in my research. Once the map has been loaded, the user might feel overwhelming stress from the serried pins. So setting the zoom level at a relatively big number could avoid the first impression being uncomfortable and stressful.

Changes & Improvements

Approach

1. Clickable buttons’ position and style are changed to provide more space for the map, and cleaner view.

2. I removed the total number of showing trails, because all the trails are already illustrating on the map, knowing the number of the trails won’t impact how user search or browse trails. It is an unnecessary information, and it doesn’t worth to give a full-width space to it on the first page of this app.

3. In the detail page, I reduced the heaviness of the information displaying and rearrange the order. The feature pitch words could give user clear and useful info in a shorter time compared to reading the paragraph of description. Besides that, I change the style of those keywords. The original style looks more like buttons, however, those words are not clickable. I am using thin lines to divide them and giving the white space for each of them for a compelling reading experience.
