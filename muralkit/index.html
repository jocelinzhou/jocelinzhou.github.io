In this section, I will explain the Target Audience, Personas and User Scenarios.

Target Audience

Primary Target Audience Overview

The primary target audience of Mural Kit is house owners, who need to paint rooms and have basic wall-painting knowledge. They are willing to contribute their leisure time to craft some useful pieces to enrich their lifes. Their minds are open to trying a new type of arts and handicrafts, and they’re comfortable with using brushes for drawing. The cost would also be one of the considerations.

Demographic

Age: 26 – 38
Gender: male/female
Hobby: DIY/ art and craft/ watching makeover TV shows, doodling
Lifestyle: optimistic/organic
Education: high school – college
Goal: self-study/ having a better room/stimulating creativity

Secondary Target Audience Overview

It would be people who operate a small business and studio. They want to engage some meaningful also pleasing decoration into their business spaces to impress their clients, visitors and business patterns.

Demographic

Age: 30-45
Gender: male/female
Occupation: small business owners
Lifestyle: organic
Education: college/above
Goal: enhance unique business features/impressive visitors

In this section, I will show the analysis of the three competitor apps.

Competitor 1/3

iDeal

itunes.apple.com/app/walltat/id419273158?mt=8

Description

iDecal is a app that allows you create your own mural from photos, design a mural decals and share your creations via Social Media.

Function

  • wall capture
  • room sample picture
  • size adjustment
  • changeable color palette
  • multiple decals
  • designed decals purchase

Visual Design

The style is quite outmoded, and the decals provided in the library are also monochromatic. The app looks like just want to sell out whatever the deals are, and make the purchase done.

PROs

  • share through email.
  • buy the selected mural.

CONs

  • only black and white customization options for photos.
  • the main purpose is for selling the decals, no tips for mural decal applying.
  • murals are unscalable on the default preview-wall, and without dimension recorded when saving the mural.
  • problems appear during use.

Competitor 2/3

ColorSnap

itunes.apple.com/app/colorsnap-visualizer-for-iphone/id316256242?mt=8

Description

ColorSnap Visualizer easily matches the images that users take with their iPhone to Sherwin-Williams paints and helps users quickly locate any color inside Sherwin-Williams’ stores. Perfect for people who are exploring the best color choice for house repainting.

Function

  • color number scanning
  • color matching with photos
  • color exploration
  • save previous tries
  • find a store

Visual Design

Overall, the whole UI and UX feel smooth, I would say it is a user-friendly app. The navigation maximizes the flexibility that allows users to know the choices and reach more through those possibilities.

PROs

  • very functional
  • detailed information when going deep
  • cooperates very well with brand-service

CONs

  • default room pictures for preview
  • monochromatic wall preview
  • too many choices to have a clear direction

Competitor 3/3

Interior Home Paint 101

itunes.apple.com/fr/app/interior-home-paint-101-diy/id1030900547?l=en&mt=8

Description

Handbook for people who want to learn how to paint like a pro at home and DIY Tips from professionals.

Visual Design

The UI design is not really good. First of all, there is very little style consistency showing in this app. The font family, color theme and icons are very different in each section. This is a tutorial app, however, it is more like a digital book with limited keyword searching function, which is written by multiple authors. From the UX perspective, there is only one navigation bar, and there is no filter to find typical tips for a technique.

PROs

  • very detailed information for sepecific painting needs
  • audio playing is availble for article parties

CONs

  • limited navigation for finding wanted content
  • not free
  • unskillful keyword searching
  • low fidelity UI and style is not consistent
  • no filter for any section

I define the whole look and feel of this project in flat design as simple, practical and intuitive. Muralkit should carry the principle of contemporary, concise with a simple design, contrasting color, and visual hierarchy. That is the mood board I collected to express the visual identities and design methods.

So I used a different level of black to fill the background, and the hight saturation yellow and the contrast color purple to be the special colors.

Interview with Mural Artists

I have interviewed two mural artists, one is a mural expert, another is an art student who was the first time to paint on large canvas. The interviews helped me to notice the difference in needs and working process from both perspectives. Also, they have given me a bunch of useful tips.

Interview with Yuan Gao

  • Art student
  • Good at hand drawing
  • First time painting on large scale canvas

This project is painted for a De Angelis Furniture shop, they need background board for shop window display. She was working with a team of another 2 artists. It is a 9-foot by 5-foot size board, they used 4 color of paints and one day hard-working to finish this project.

Tips from Yuan:

  • Prepare well before starting painting.
  • Don’t use too many colors.
  • Use a pencil to briefly sketch out outlines.
  • Fill in spots that use the same color at one time, it will reduce the chance of getting messy.
  • If it dose get messy, let the previous layer dry, then repaint with the correct color.

Interview with Victor Reyes

  • Professional painter and muralist
  • Located in San Francisco

http://reyes78.com/

This mural, Spray Enamel, and acrylic are located in Mission distraction, it is 50’ x 30’. He spent 3 days to finish it from the sketch.

Victor’s blueprint creating process.

  1. .pencil sketch
  2.  illustrator
  3. projecting

Tips from Victor:

  • Painting spots in the same color at one time to avoid dealing with a dry brush.
  • Don’t stop too often, otherwise, you’ll lose all the passion of the moment.
  • Keep the number of colors to 2,6 or 10.
  • If confident, mix color with white paint to get different levels of saturation.

First Round Testing

I started with interviewing people who are in the range of my target audience. They filled out the questionnaire and tested the first version of the paper prototypes.

Problems figured out from this round:

  • Users basic and critical needs.
  • Illustrate menu in a clear way.
  • Type of mural that suite for beginners’ skill level.
  • Most necessary technique for wall painting but not well known by beginner painters.
  • The reasons that drag them back from getting started.

Second Round Testing

At this stage, I used a clickable prototype to test with users.

Problems figured out from this round:

  • The number of paints that good for beginners to start with
  • Paint information that painter is needed, familiar with and ready-to-use.
  • The improved UI that allow users to explore, add and remove colors.
  • The layout of a budget page.
  • Tutorial page layout twisting.

Third Round Testing

I was using my coded prototype to test with users.

Problems figured out from this round:

  • Not well-fitted bugs.
  • Visual issues.
